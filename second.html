Thousands of animals are killed due to noise pollution.

DISRUPTION

Ocean noise pollution comes from harmful and disruptive sounds made by human activities such as commercial shipping, seismic surveys, oil exploration, military sonar and construction.

DOLPHINS

They adapt to noise pollution by altering their vocalisation, behaviour and social cohesion, such as increasing their frequency, migration pattern and by staying together.

CRUSTACEANS

Low-frequency sounds can cause injuries and even death.

SEALS

High frequency sounds deafens seals making them impossible to hunt, navigate and communicate.

SHARKS

Continuous loud noises cause stress responses in endocrine and other systems that can have long term health effects.

WHALES

High intensity nosie can disorient their navigation systems, echolocation and leads them to shallow waters where they get trapped and die.
